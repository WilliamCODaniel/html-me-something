Captains

Shark

Shark is THE goal threat captain in all of Guild Ball. Whether kicking off or receiving, Shark has the tools to score from almost anywhere on the pitch. He wants the ball and the glory. With the right combination of Squaddies, a Shark led team is most likely to get a 3 goal win.

Corsair

Corsair is the beater of the Fisherman's Guild. A Corsair led squad will be a little more focused on takeouts than goals, while still being flexible enough to get a score or two. Corsair wants to control his opponents movements via a variety of abilities that draw them in. Add Kraken to a Corsair team and you've got a strong one-two punch.

Yukai

Yukai is the new Captain on the wharf, having only been availible since March, 2019. Yukai is more akin to Shark than Corsair yet feels incredibly unique when on the pitch. Yukai wants to coach their squad to victory by passing out buffs and, in the case of their Legendary Play, extreme repositioning. A Yukai led team will mainly focus on goals and will rarely try to do much else. Bring Fathom and Angel along for a potential 2 goal activation!

Squaddies

Angel

If Shark is the superstar striker Captain, then Angel is the Squaddie equivalent. She can score from almost anywhere and is very difficult to hit thanks to a 5+ DEF. She's a MUST in a Yukai led squad.

Greyscales

Greyscales is the elder statesman of the Fisherman's Guild. He is a good addition to most squads thanks to his "Ball's Gone" character play and unusual movement characteristics, specifically "Unpredictable Movement" which allows him to get away from engagements.

Hag

Before Yukai there was Hag. She is basically a command and control Squaddie, moving her teammates around at will with "Fisher's Reel". She is good at staying out of trouble while getting her teammates into proper positions on the pitch. A good addition to a Shark or Corsair-led squad.

Jac

Jac is a great addition to a Corsair-captained squad thanks to his access to "Battering Ram" and "Goad". His playbook is well suited to getting enemies into positions for Gang-Ups due to the easy access to Push results. Sakana is a generally preferred option to Jac, but Jac still has his uses.

Kraken

Kraken recently received a buff and is now well-suited to be a part of any Fisherman squad. He is able to pull enemies in with "Drag" and once they are engaging him, they can't target anyone else! He is fairly hardy too thanks to the "Tough Hide" ability and his 1 armor. He is now very close to the must-include category of Fish Squaddies.

Sakana

Considered the ideal squaddie by some, Sakana is a welcome addition to pretty much any Fisherman lineup. He is very well rounded, with a decent Kick stat and good mobility. When the game comes to blows, he's very capable thanks to his "Anatomical Precision" trait that allows him to ignore 1 armor. Throw in a free Counter Attack once per turn, and he becomes quite formidible.

Veteran Sakana

Veteran Sakana has seen more play recently thanks to the increased focus on the kicking game overall in Guild Ball. V. Sakana still has "Anatomical Precision" which makes him good in a fight, but he now comes with the ability to pass out an extra Influence to a teammate via "Bag of Coffers".

Siren

Siren may not have a lot of health, but she is more than capable of holding her own. She has seen a recent decline in lineups thanks to "Seduced" having it's ranged lowerd. However, she is still able to do enough controlling of the other team that she warrants consideration. If you do include Siren, Kraken must come along for the ride thanks to her "Protected" ability, which gives her +1 armor when Kraken is close.

Veteran Siren

Veteran Siren usually sees the pitch more than her non-Vet variant these days. Her "Dread Gaze" ability is very useful for limiting opponents' ability to move, thanks to a knockdown. She is better at surviving this time around thanks to "Escaping Fate" which allows her to go back to 1 HP after being reduced to 0 and make a 2" dodge to get out of harms way.

Fathom*

Fathom is actually a part of the Fisherman's Guild minor Guild, The Navigator's. She is an excellent striker and a must-have Squaddie on a team with Yukai at the helm. If everything goes right, a team with Fathom and Angel can score back-to-back goals before the other team has a chance to activate! Throw in an ability that allows her to clear conditions and you have an excellent addition to any Fish squad (but especially Yukai).

Horizon*

Horizon is also a member of the Navigator's Guild. He is best used as a red-herring to the other team due to his "Don't Get Cocky" ability, which only gives the other team 1 Victory Point for taking him out. He is quite mobile with a once per turn 5" dodge that could lead to some sneaky scoring.

Mascots

Salt

Salt is the sneaky goal threat of the Fisherman mascots. If she can get into position, which isn't too difficult given her 17" threat range! Definitlely the mascot of choice with Shark as the Lead.

Tentacles

Tentacles is to Salt what Corsair is to Shark. Two very different styles of play but enough goal threat to keep things interesting. Tentacles' "Blind" ability is very sueful for keeping the other team from accomplishing it's plan. Then throw in "Close Control" and you've got a mascot that can (and should) be holding the ball.
